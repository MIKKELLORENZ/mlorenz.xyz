# Self-Watering Planter

A 3D-printed desk planter with a built-in capillary wick system. Designed for people who love plants but forget to water them.

## The Problem

I kept killing desk plants. Not out of malice — just forgetfulness. Every self-watering planter I found online was either ugly, expensive, or both. So I designed my own.

## How It Works

The planter has two chambers:

- **Upper chamber** — where the plant and soil sit
- **Lower reservoir** — holds about two weeks' worth of water

A cotton wick connects the two. Capillary action pulls water up into the soil as it dries out. Simple physics, no electronics, no pumps.

![Diagram of the wick system](diagram.png)

## Design Decisions

### Material

I used PETG instead of PLA because it handles moisture better long-term. PLA tends to degrade and get brittle when constantly damp.

### Drainage

There's a small overflow hole in the reservoir wall so you can't accidentally flood it. The water level is visible through a small transparent window on the side.

## Bill of Materials

1. PETG filament (~50g per planter)
2. Cotton rope, 4mm diameter
3. Small piece of clear acrylic for the window
4. Silicone sealant for the window joint

## Results

Three months in and the basil plant on my desk is still alive. That's a personal record.
