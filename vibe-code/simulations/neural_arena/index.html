How it works

This is the battle-only sibling of Neural Racers: no laps, no finish line — fitness is survival. Each car senses walls and cover with 9 distance rays, plus its own speed, reload and hull state. Enemies are sensed only with line of sight: duck behind cover and you vanish from rivals' senses — and since bullets stop at walls too, hiding is real, not cosmetic. But a sighting leaves a fading memory trace: for a few seconds a car still knows roughly where the enemy it lost went, which is what makes stalking, peeking and ambushing learnable. A car also senses whether the enemy it sees is aiming at it (dodge fuel) and how damaged it is (finish the weak, avoid the strong). Teammates are always sensed via radio, including their hull state, and incoming bullets are heard at long range. Everything feeds a small recurrent neural network that outputs steering, throttle and trigger.

Cars take 3 hits to destroy, and every landed hit pays the shooter and stings the victim — fitness flows from skirmishes, not just confirmed kills, which makes marksmanship evolvable and cuts the luck out of selection. The killing blow pays extra, and anyone who recently softened the target up earns an assist. Hull damage slowly repairs after a few unhit seconds, so pulling back behind cover to heal is a real tactic. Near misses earn graded credit, firing blind costs a little, and grinding against a wall drains fitness at an accelerating rate — getting stuck is bred out, hiding is not. Camping still has a clock: partway through each round a danger zone closes toward the arena center, and lingering outside it is fatal.

Free-for-all is every car for itself with one gene pool. Team battle pits red against blue with two gene pools co-evolving against each other. A subtlety: selection happens within each team's pool, so a bonus shared equally by the whole team cancels out and selects for nothing — cooperation only evolves through rewards that land on individuals, like assists for focus fire and surviving a won round. Friendly fire is on and costly, so trigger discipline has to evolve along with aggression.

Training is neuroevolution: each generation fights three rounds from fresh spawn points (averaging out luck), and the fittest seed the next generation with mutated copies of their network weights. Expect hundreds of generations at high speed before clear tactics show — watch for wounded cars breaking off to heal, pairs pushing together, and ambushes at corners. Turn on Eval mode to freeze learning and test the evolved brains on other arenas or the other mode.
