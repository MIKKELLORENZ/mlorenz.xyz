How it works

Each lander flies with the same control rules as the original moon lander: gravity is 27, the main engine produces 65 thrust that grows as fuel burns off, the side jets produce 27 thrust, and the lander only survives contact if its vertical speed stays under 57 and its angle stays within 30 degrees.

The sensor stack mirrors your brief. The brain sees 7 downward terrain rays plus a short ray-memory snapshot, 3 landing-pad neurons when an unclaimed pad is within one viewport of range, and extra state for local velocity, ground-relative motion, acceleration, a dedicated terrain-relative clearance scalar, oxygen, fuel, and recent thruster commands.

Reinforcement learning uses reward-shaped selection to keep the best brain, mix it with other high performers, and inject some fresh individuals. Evolutionary algorithm is the pure no-gradient mode: the champion survives exactly, 5-7 mutated champion variants appear every generation, fresh random individuals are always added, and crossover children inherit direct weight mixes from the champion and lower-ranked survivors.
